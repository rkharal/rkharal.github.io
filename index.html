High-Performance GPU Systems Engineer & Researcher

Rosina Kharal

I build and study high-performance GPU systems, with a focus on dynamic data structures, hardware-accelerated indexing, and rigorous performance engineering.

  • PhD researcher in GPU-accelerated data structures, dynamic indexing, and massively parallel systems.
  • Hands-on development with CUDA, OptiX, C, and C++, including cooperative groups, warp-level primitives, atomics, and synchronization.
  • Kernel-level optimization through profiling, memory-access analysis, occupancy tuning, and contention reduction.
  • Designer of automated, large-scale benchmark campaigns evaluating throughput, latency, scalability, and memory efficiency across diverse workloads.
  • Strong computer architecture and systems foundation, supported by publications, cross-institution research, and technical mentorship.
